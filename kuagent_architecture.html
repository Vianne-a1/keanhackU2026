01 — Problem

Policy Confusion

Employees often do not know which company policies apply before they buy software, share customer data, travel, or work with vendors. The policies exist, but they are long, scattered, and difficult to interpret.

Vendor Contract Risk

Vendor contracts may include hidden fees, unfair renewal clauses, unusual payment terms, or suspicious bank account changes. These risks are easy to miss during fast internal approvals.

03 — Management Flow: Company Side

1

Document Upload

Admins upload company policy documents such as procurement, IT security, travel, and data sharing policies.

2

Text Extraction

The backend extracts clean text from PDF or DOCX files using document parsers.

3

Chunking

Long policy documents are split into smaller searchable sections.

4

Vectorization

Each chunk is converted into embeddings so the system can search by meaning, not just keywords.

5

Scoped Storage

Chunks and vectors are stored with company IDs so one company can never access another company’s data.

04 — Assistant Flow: User Side

1

Ask Question

Employee asks: “Can I buy this software with my company card?”

2

Intent Detection

The agent identifies whether this is about procurement, IT approval, vendor risk, or data sharing.

3

Semantic Search

Relevant policy sections are retrieved from that company’s private vector database.

4

Policy Reasoning

The agent compares rules across multiple policies before making a decision.

5

Final Verdict

The user receives Approved, Needs Approval, Prohibited, or Red Flag with plain-English reasoning.

05 — Agent Pipeline Detail

Agent 1

Policy Retriever

Searches company-specific policy chunks using semantic search. Returns the most relevant sections with source titles and citations.

Agent 2

Policy Reasoning Agent

Checks multiple policies together. Example: software purchase may require procurement approval, IT security approval, and vendor review.

Agent 3

Fraud Detection Agent

Scans uploaded vendor contracts for ghost services, unusual payment terms, bank account changes, and mismatch from standard templates.

Agent 4

Fairness Audit Agent

Detects predatory clauses such as automatic long-term renewal, no opt-out window, hidden fees, or one-sided termination terms.

06 — Verdict System

Approved

The action follows policy requirements. The user can proceed without additional approval.

Needs Approval

The action may be allowed, but requires manager, legal, procurement, or IT security approval.

Prohibited / Red Flag

The action violates company policy or the contract contains suspicious or unfair terms.
